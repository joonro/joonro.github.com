Research Statement¶

Joon Ro
The University of Texas at Austin
McCombs School of Business
Marketing Department

My main research interest is evaluating the effectiveness of firms’ marketing strategies in the setting where dynamic components such as forward-looking consumers, secondary market, and network effects are important. I found it fascinating how these factors can significantly alter the effectiveness of the marketing strategies, and failure to take these into account often leads to wrong policy implications.

I study these issues by applying empirical industrial organization models to marketing; I employ structural models and conduct counterfactual analysis to evaluate marketing and economic policies. I enjoy tackling computationally challenging problems and implementing efficient algorithms. I am passionate about reproducible science, and I have released a couple of scientific libraries that I have developed for my projects as open source software packages.

For example, .. begin-essay-1 I develop a dynamic structural model of durable goods, incorporating both demand and supply side under the presence of used goods market in the first essay of my dissertation. This model incorporates firm’s optimal intertemporal pricing, forward-looking consumers, and their interaction with the secondary market. By exploiting firm’s optimal policy, this model can recover demand parameters by only using prices, without observing sales information. I apply this model to video games industry, and estimate game-specific demand. With estimated parameters, I evaluate several marketing and economic policies by counterfactual analysis, notably the evaluation of the profit after elimination of the secondary market.

In the subsequent essay, .. begin-essay-2 I consider additional marketing strategy of post-release add-on. Many producers in the video game industry releases add-ons called downloadable contents (DLC). The idea of this paper is that this add-on can be an effective strategy to reduce the competition from the used goods market. This is because in order to play the DLC, a consumer must have the original copy of the game. Hence, if consumers expect future release of the add-ons, ones who want to play the DLC will hold on to their disc instead of selling them, effectively reducing the used copy supply. I plan to extend the model of the first essay and add the release of DLC as another control variable. Counterfactual analysis will shed light on the profit implications and optimal release timing of the DLC.

In another project work in progress, I estimate aggregate dynamic demand with random coefficient model to quantify the welfare and profit implications of bilateral free trade agreement. Recently South Korea has negotiated a couple of major bilateral free trade agreements (FTAs), with EU and the U.S, which have come into force in 2011 and 2012, respectively. Since there are significant time gap between the announcement of an agreement and the actual date when the agreement enters into effect, durable goods consumers may postpone purchase, expecting future price decline due to the agreement. Thus modeling dynamics is important in the welfare and profit calculation. Utilizing unique, hand-collected automotive characteristics information and aggregate level sales data, I plan to estimate dynamic new durable goods demand model and conduct counterfactual analyses with different levels of tariffs. This will shed light on the durable goods industry dynamics due to competition environment change.

The policy change of FTAs will also give me an opportunity to study multi-product firms’ product introduction decision. There have been significant entries of new models in the Korean automotive market after the FTAs’. This will provide information about supply side policy and I plan to estimate oblivious equilibrium model of imperfect competition.

As a researcher, I am committed to application of rigorous methodology to relevant problems. By studying the effectiveness of marketing policies under different market environments I aim to provide both managerial implications and policy recommendations. I believe an empirical researcher’s ability to accommodate more realistic models and the range of questions he can address critically depend on his proficiency in computation, and I look to improve my skills continuously. As a Marketing researcher, I strive to work on issues that has significant substantial and methodological contributions.
