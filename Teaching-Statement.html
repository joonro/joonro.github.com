My teaching interests include Principles of Marketing, Marketing Research, Pricing, and Data Mining. I have taught an undergraduate course (the Principles of Marketing) and was nominated for Fred Moore Assistant Instructor Awards for Teaching Excellence. In addition, I have been working as teaching assistant for various undergraduate courses as well as an PhD course.

I believe it is very important to highlight the relevance of the class for students’ motivation to learn. First, I have clear objectives for the class listed on the syllabus. Also, I explain the position of the class in the relation to other classes (i.e., what are the subsequent classes they could take and how taking this class will help them in those classes). The relevance of each topic will also be made clear by highlighting the position of the topic in the big picture of the whole course. In addition, I describe how specific course content can be applied in their potential future occupations. Another way of showing students the relevance of the class is through guest speakers. I generally include 2-3 guest speaker sessions during a semester, where people from industry describe their jobs and show how the marketing knowledge we learn during the class is being applied in the real world.

I emphasize applications of the course content. First, I have frequent, small in-class exercises. These are often group exercises, and students are asked to apply concepts they learned during the class to a small but real-world problem, which are usually introduced through business articles from business periodicals such as Wall Street Journal and Financial Times. This is meant to remind them of the applicability of the subject. Also, I require an end of semester group project. Through the in-class exercises students practice applying one concept. The group project builds on this work as they practice integrating what they have learned throughout the course into one project.

I try to distill the knowledge and organize the material in a way that students could see the structure of the knowledge. In addition, I try to incorporate various multimedia (e.g., commercial videos which show promotion strategies; audio clips from radio business programs which describe new business opportunities) into the class. Students have different learning styles, and it will enhances their learning since they will be exposed presentation of ideas in different formats. This also ensures repeated exposure to concepts and helps keeping them engaged during class sessions.
