Teaching¶

My teaching interests include Marketing Research, Marketing Analytics, and Digital Marketing along with other core courses. I have taught an undergraduate course (Principles of Marketing) and was nominated for Fred Moore Assistant Instructor Awards for Teaching Excellence with average evaluation 4.0/5.0. To advance my teaching abilities, I took a graduate teaching scholars seminar and participated in a teaching study group hosted by Software Carpentry. In addition, I have worked as a teaching assistant for various undergraduate courses (Principles of Marketing, Marketing Information and Analysis) as well as a PhD course (Bayesian Econometrics). Also, I will be a teaching assistant for a master’s level Marketing Analytics course next semester, one of the electives of McCombs’ Business Analytics program. I volunteered for this position to better prepare myself to teach an analytics course.

I want to provide engaging learning experiences to students through teaching. Furthermore, I want to make a long-term impact in their career by ensuring to deliver relevant materials and also taking extra attention in their development. Specifically, I want my students to understand how the materials they learned during classes can be applied in the real-world and to be able to teach themselves when needed, and to become life-long, self-regulated learners. I emphasize relevance, digestibility, applicability, and development in teaching.

Relevance¶

It is important to highlight the relevance of a class to enhance students’ motivation to learn. Thus, I have clear and concrete objectives of the course in the syllabus. Second, I explain the position of the class in relation to other classes (i.e., what the subsequent classes they could take are and how taking this class will help them in those). Third, the importance of each topic or chapter is made clear by highlighting its role in the big picture of the entire course. Fourth, I describe how specific course content can be applied in their potential future occupations. Finally, I invite guest speakers to show students the relevance of the class; during a semester I generally invite 2-3 guest speakers who are currently active in a relevant industry, so they can share their experiences and answer students’ questions about how the marketing knowledge learned in classes is being applied in the real world.

Digestibility¶

I try to distill the concepts and organize class materials in ways that students could clearly see the structure of the knowledge being delivered. For example, each class starts with review of the materials we learn from the last session and then the intro of the current one. The class ends with summary of the materials just covered, emphasizing how the current topic is connected to other topics and its place in the long-term goals of the class. In addition, I try to incorporate various multimedia (e.g., commercial videos for various promotion strategies, audio clips from radio business programs describing new business opportunities) into the class. Students have different learning styles, and exposure to several formats of learning materials can enhance the teaching effectiveness. This also ensures repeated exposure to concepts and helps keeping students engaged during classes.

Application¶

I emphasize applications of course content. First, I frequently engage students in exercises. These are typically group activities, and students are asked to apply concepts they learned during the class to a small but real-world problem; the problems are usually introduced through business articles from business periodicals such as the Wall Street Journal and Financial Times. This is meant to remind students of the applicability of the learned subject. Also, I require an end-of-semester group project. Through the in-class exercises students practice applying one concept; the final group project builds on this work as they practice integrating what they have learned throughout the course more comprehensively, and in a larger scale.

Development¶

I emphasize development in both students’ learning and my teaching skills. I enjoy advising and interacting with students, and I thrive to improve my teaching through incorporating students’ feedback. Students will be more motivated and persistent in learning when the teacher shows interest in their individual development and opinions. Also, I am interested in developing new teaching methods using new technologies, and my superior computer-related skills will certainly facilitate this.

My teaching philosophies and strategies summarized above reflect my belief about how individuals learn. I believe that students make the most out of a course when they are motivated, can practice application, and are able to comprehend materials fluently. Through continuously incorporating students’ and colleagues’ feedback, I believe my teaching effectiveness will only grow as I teach.
