Teaching¶

My teaching interests include Marketing Research, Marketing Analytics, and Digital Marketing along with other core courses. I have taught an undergraduate course (Principles of Marketing) and was nominated for the Fred Moore Assistant Instructor Awards for Teaching Excellence with an average evaluation of 4.0/5.0. To advance my teaching abilities, I took the graduate teaching scholars seminar and participated in instructor training hosted by Software Carpentry. In addition, I have worked as a teaching assistant for various undergraduate courses (Principles of Marketing, Marketing Information and Analysis) as well as a PhD course (Bayesian Econometrics). Also, I will be a teaching assistant for a master’s level Marketing Analytics course next semester, one of the electives of McCombs’ Business Analytics program. I volunteered for this position to better prepare myself to teach an analytics course.

I want to provide engaging learning experiences to students through teaching. Furthermore, I want to make a long-term impact in their career by delivering relevant materials and also paying careful attention to their development. Specifically, I help my students understand how the materials they learned in class can be applied in the real-world and learn how to teach themselves when needed o they can become life-long, self-regulated learners. For this I emphasize relevance, digestibility, applicability, and development in teaching.

Relevance¶

It is important to highlight the relevance of a class to enhance students’ motivation to learn. Thus, I have clear and concrete objectives of the course in the syllabus. Second, I explain the position of the class in relation to other classes (i.e., i.e., how my course opens up opportunities to take other relevant courses). Third, the importance of each topic or chapter is made clear by highlighting its role in the big picture of the entire course. Fourth, I describe how specific course content can be applied in their potential future occupations. Finally, I invite guest speakers to show students the relevance of the class; during a semester I generally invite 2-3 guest speakers who are currently active in a related industry, so the speakers can share their experiences and answer students’ questions about how the marketing knowledge learned in classes can be translated into the real world.

Digestibility¶

I try to distill the concepts and organize class materials in ways that allow students to clearly see the structure of the knowledge being delivered. For example, each class session starts with a review of the previous session which then transitions into the intro of the current one. The class ends with summary of materials covered in it, emphasizing how they are connected to other topics and help with the achievement of long-term goals of the entire course. In addition, I try to incorporate various multimedia (e.g., videos illustrating various promotion strategies, audio clips from radio business programs describing new business opportunities) into the class. Students have different learning styles, and introducing various formats of learning materials can enhance the teaching effectiveness. This also ensures repeated exposure to concepts and keeps students engaged during classes.

Application¶

I emphasize applications of course content. First, I frequently engage students in exercises. These are typically group activities, where students apply concepts they learned during the class to a small but concrete real-world problem; the problems are usually introduced through business articles from periodicals such as the Wall Street Journal and Financial Times. This is meant to remind students of the applicability of the learned subject. Also, I require an end-of-semester group project. Through the in-class exercises students practice applying one concept; the final group project builds on this work as they practice integrating what they have learned throughout the course more comprehensively, and in a larger scale.

Development¶

I emphasize development in both students’ learning and my teaching skills. Students will be more motivated and persistent in learning when the teacher shows interest in their individual development and opinions. I enjoy advising and interacting with students. Moreover, I thrive to improve my teaching through incorporating their feedback. I am also interested in developing new teaching methods using up-to-date technologies, and my superior computer-related skills will certainly facilitate this.
